Why Mobile Security Fails

Traditional antivirus apps only scan for malware. They don't protect you from the social engineering tricks used in modern mobile attacks.

Common Mobile Risks

Users are tricked into giving away data because they can't verify the intent behind everyday actions.

  • Opening fake bank verification links sent via SMS.
  • Giving camera/microphone access to a simple calculator app.
  • Ignoring complex security alerts because they lack context.

How CyberGuard Helps

We use AI to read the context of messages and permissions, giving you clear, human-readable advice.

  • Scans SMS language for urgency, OTP requests, and known phishing URLs.
  • Flags apps that request permissions completely unrelated to their function.
  • Provides an interactive AI agent to explain exactly why a threat is dangerous.

Is this message safe? "URGENT: Your Netflix account is suspended. Update payment details here: http://bit.ly/update-pay-19"
High Risk: Phishing Attempt

This message is highly suspicious. It uses urgent language ("URGENT", "suspended") to create panic. The link provided is a shortened URL (bit.ly) which official companies like Netflix rarely use for billing issues. Do not click the link or provide any payment details.
