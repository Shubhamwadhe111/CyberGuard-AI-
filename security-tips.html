⭐ Tip of the Day

Your bank will NEVER ask for your OTP over a call or SMS

Legitimate financial institutions verify through their official app only. If someone asks for your OTP — hang up immediately and call your bank's official number.

Ask AI
Critical Phishing

Recognize phishing attempts instantly

Look for urgency ("Act NOW!"), unknown senders, misspelled brand names, and requests for personal data. Legitimate companies never pressure you via SMS.

Ask AI
CriticalPhishing

Never share OTP or passwords

No bank, government body, or company will ever call or text you asking for your OTP, PIN, or password. Anyone who does is a scammer.

Ask AI
HighLinks

Never click unknown shortened URLs

Links like bit.ly or tinyurl hide the real destination. Always use our URL Checker before clicking any unfamiliar link received via SMS or chat.

Check URL
HighLinks

Check HTTPS before entering data

Always verify the padlock icon and "https://" in the URL. Never enter personal info on HTTP sites — your data travels unencrypted.

Ask AI
HighApps

Audit app permissions monthly

A Calculator app has no business accessing your microphone, contacts, or GPS. Go to Settings → Apps → Permissions and revoke anything suspicious.

App Audit
MediumApps

Only install from official stores

Sideloading APK files bypasses Google's security checks entirely. Only download apps from the Play Store or App Store where apps are reviewed.

Ask AI
CriticalAccount

Enable 2FA on every critical account

Two-factor authentication blocks 99% of automated attacks. Use an authenticator app (Google Authenticator, Authy) instead of SMS-based 2FA for stronger security.

Ask AI
HighAccount

Use unique passwords for every account

Reusing passwords means one breach exposes all your accounts. Use a password manager (Bitwarden, 1Password) to generate and store strong unique passwords.

Ask AI
HighNetwork

Avoid public Wi-Fi for sensitive tasks

Public Wi-Fi can be monitored. Never do banking, shopping, or login to accounts on public Wi-Fi. Use mobile data or a VPN for protection.

Ask AI
MediumNetwork

Use a VPN on public networks

A VPN encrypts your connection and masks your IP address, protecting you from man-in-the-middle attacks on unsecured networks like airport or café Wi-Fi.

Ask AI
HighDevice

Keep OS and apps always updated

Security patches fix known vulnerabilities that attackers actively exploit. Enable automatic updates for your OS and all installed apps.

Ask AI
MediumDevice

Enable biometric screen lock

Use fingerprint or face unlock combined with a strong PIN backup. This prevents unauthorized physical access if your device is lost or stolen.

Ask AI
Daily Challenge
Check all your app permissions today

Go to Settings → Apps and revoke any permission that seems unnecessary for each app's function.
