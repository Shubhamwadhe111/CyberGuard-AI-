The Golden Rule of Mobile Security

Your bank or financial institution will never ask for your password, PIN, or One-Time Passcode (OTP) over SMS or a phone call. Always use the official mobile app to verify any suspicious account activity.

Important

Never share OTP or passwords

Scammers use fake urgent messages to trick you into providing your authentication codes. Keep them private.

Important

Avoid unknown links

Do not click on shortened URLs (like bit.ly) or links from senders you don't recognize. They often lead to phishing sites.

Recommended

Review app permissions regularly

A flashlight app shouldn't need access to your microphone or contacts. Revoke permissions that don't make sense.

Recommended

Keep your phone updated

OS updates patch critical security vulnerabilities. Enable automatic updates to stay protected against new exploits.

Basic

Use official apps only

Download apps exclusively from the Google Play Store or Apple App Store. Sideloading APKs bypasses security checks.

Basic

Enable screen lock

Use a strong PIN, password, or biometric lock to prevent physical access if your device is lost or stolen.

Important

Turn on two-factor authentication

Add an extra layer of security to your critical accounts. If a password is compromised, 2FA prevents unauthorized access.

Recommended

Report suspicious messages

If you receive a phishing text, block the sender and report it to your carrier to help protect others.
